# Hospital Policy: Code Blue Response Protocol

## Policy Number: HOS-EM-001
## Effective Date: 2024-01-01
## Review Date: 2025-01-01
## Department: Emergency Medicine

## Purpose
To establish a standardized, coordinated response to cardiac or respiratory 
arrest events throughout the hospital, ensuring optimal patient outcomes through 
rapid, efficient, and effective resuscitation efforts.

## Scope
This policy applies to all clinical staff, including physicians, nurses, respiratory 
therapists, and support personnel in all patient care areas.

## Definitions
- **Code Blue**: Emergency code indicating cardiac or respiratory arrest requiring immediate resuscitation
- **RRT**: Rapid Response Team - specialized team for patients deteriorating before arrest
- **AED**: Automated External Defibrillator
- **CPR**: Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation

## Policy Statement
All hospital staff must respond immediately to Code Blue activation. The designated
 Code Blue Team shall assume responsibility for patient resuscitation, with clear roles and communication protocols.

## Procedures

### 1. Code Blue Activation
- **When to Activate**: 
  - Patient unresponsive with no pulse or respirations
  - Witnessed cardiac or respiratory arrest
  - Clinical deterioration with immediate threat to life
- **Activation Method**:
  - Dial hospital emergency number: 5555
  - Announce "Code Blue" with location
  - Press Code Blue button in patient rooms (if available)
  - Use hospital communication system

### 2. Initial Responder Responsibilities (First 30 Seconds)
- Verify unresponsiveness and check for pulse
- Begin CPR immediately if no pulse
- Call for help using emergency system
- Retrieve nearest AED or crash cart
- Assign someone to meet Code Blue Team

### 3. Code Blue Team Composition
| Role | Staff | Responsibilities |
|------|-------|------------------|
| Team Leader | Attending Physician | Directs resuscitation, makes clinical decisions |
| Airway Manager | Anesthesiologist/RT | Manages airway, intubation, ventilation |
| Compressor 1 | Nurse/Resident | Performs chest compressions |
| Compressor 2 | Nurse/Resident | Rotates with Compressor 1 every 2 minutes |
| Defibrillator | Nurse/Technician | Operates defibrillator, prepares medications |
| Recorder | Nurse | Documents events, medications, rhythms |
| Medication | Pharmacist | Prepares and administers medications |
| Support | Nursing Staff | Obtains equipment, communicates with family |

### 4. Resuscitation Algorithm
- **Initial Assessment** (first 2 minutes):
  - Confirm cardiac arrest
  - Begin high-quality CPR (100-120 compressions/min, depth 5-6 cm)
  - Attach defibrillator pads
  - Analyze rhythm

- **Shockable Rhythms** (VF/pVT):
  - Defibrillate at 200J biphasic
  - Resume CPR immediately for 2 minutes
  - Administer epinephrine 1mg every 3-5 minutes
  - Consider amiodarone 300mg after third shock

- **Non-Shockable Rhythms** (asystole/PEA):
  - Continue CPR
  - Administer epinephrine 1mg every 3-5 minutes
  - Identify and treat reversible causes (5 H's and 5 T's)

### 5. Reversible Causes (5 H's and 5 T's)
| Category | Condition | Intervention |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| H | Hypovolemia | Fluid resuscitation |
| H | Hypoxia | Ensure adequate ventilation |
| H | Hydrogen ion (acidosis) | Correct pH, consider bicarbonate |
| H | Hyper/hypokalemia | Correct electrolytes |
| H | Hypothermia | Rewarming |
| T | Tension pneumothorax | Needle decompression |
| T | Tamponade (cardiac) | Pericardiocentesis |
| T | Toxins | Antidotes, supportive care |
| T | Thrombosis (pulmonary) | Thrombolytics, thrombectomy |
| T | Thrombosis (coronary) | PCI, thrombolytics |

### 6. Post-Resuscitation Care
- Transfer to ICU or appropriate unit
- Targeted temperature management for comatose patients
- Continuous monitoring for 24 hours minimum
- Neurological assessment every 2 hours
- Debriefing for Code Blue Team within 24 hours
- Complete Code Blue documentation in electronic health record

### 7. Equipment and Supplies
- Crash carts located on every nursing unit
- Daily checks of all equipment
- AEDs in all public areas
- Backup equipment available in central supply

## Documentation Requirements
- Code Blue Record form
- Time of each event (arrest, CPR start, shocks, medications)
- Team member names and roles
- Patient outcome (ROSC, transfer, mortality)
- Debriefing notes

## Training Requirements
- BLS certification: Annual renewal for all clinical staff
- ACLS certification: Biennial renewal for Code Blue Team members
- Mock code drills: Quarterly for all units
- New employee orientation: Within 30 days of hire

## Quality Improvement
- Review all Code Blue events monthly
- Track key metrics:
  - Time to first defibrillation
  - ROSC rate
  - Survival to discharge
  - Time to team arrival
- Implement corrective actions for identified gaps
